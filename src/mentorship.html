1-on-1 Coding Mentorship

What to Expect

The 1-on-1 mentoring program takes you from beginner to software engineer through my tried-and-true formula:

Getting Started

  • First you'll take an assessment to make sure it's a good fit for both of us.
  • Our first session together is to decide on a path and timeline that works for you. (See the standard curriculum.)

Doing the Work

  • Next, we figure out a meeting schedule. The recommended schedule is 2 sessions per week, 1 hour per session.
  • You will have access to a private Slack server for messaging me anytime.
  • Between sessions, you are expected to put in the work
    • part-time (20 hours per week), or
    • full-time (40 hours per week).
  • As you work through the curriculum, I will meet with you to:
    • Review work, answer any questions, and assign new goals.
    • Plan your next project.
    • Provide a specific topic lesson (as needed).
  • Session goals are flexible. If you want to learn about problem solving and data structures, we can fit it in!

Applying for Jobs

  • When you're ready to enter the market, sessions will cover:
    • Interview practice
    • Career services help
    • Knowledge gaps

Curriculum

This is the standard path I recommend to anyone interested in becoming a web developer. I developed this curriculum over time, based on my institutional and private teaching experience. Most of the work is project-focused or hands-on exercises. This curriculum was planned with the idea of balancing industry needs and building strong foundational skills that you can carry throughout your career. Although the curriculum has a lot of structure and detail, one of the benefits of working with an experienced mentor is having the flexibility to change the curriculum based on personal interests and current trends. At the end of the day, there is no ideal path. It all comes down to making valuable use of your time and moving forward.

The estimated times below refer to student working time, not time spent meeting with a mentor.
Total estimated time: 368 hours.
At a part-time pace (20 hours per week), that's about 18 weeks.
At a full-time pace (40 hours per week), that's about 9 weeks.
