Private Mentoring

Thinking about changing careers?

(rough) I offer private mentoring as a service. I use a slack server to keep in touch with my students. The recommended schedule is meeting twice a week, 1 hour each session. A typical session involves any of:

  • Reviewing work, answering any questions, assigning goals
  • Planning a project
  • Specific topic lesson (as needed)
As your mentor, my role is to help you with:
  • planning your projects,
  • having a good sense of reassurance that you're learning well and on the right path,
  • knowing what your next steps are,
  • and being available for you to ask questions and get good explanations when you're stuck.
I advocate for project-focused learning wherever possible, so you will be learning real-world techniques and have a great portfolio by the end.

Contact me for a free consultation.

Curriculum

(rough) This is the standard path I would recommend to anyone interested in becoming a web developer. I developed this curriculum over time, based on institutional and private teaching experience. The estimated hours refer to student working time, not mentor meeting time. You could work through this curriculum alone, if you wanted to. Of course, one of the benefits of working with an experienced mentor is having the flexibility to change the curriculum based on personal interests and current trends. Project-focused, hands-on. At the end of the day, there is no ideal curriculum. It all comes down to making valuable use of your time and staying on a forward path.
